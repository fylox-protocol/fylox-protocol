# Fylox Protocol Layers

Last updated: 2026

Fylox is designed as a **modular protocol**. The system is planned to evolve in **five conceptual layers**.  
The initial MVP focuses only on the core payment interaction, but the full architecture is envisioned as follows:

---

## Layer 1 – Core Payment Infrastructure

- Handles **basic payment requests** and confirmations
- QR-based transactions for Pi Network
- Minimal Viable Product (MVP) testing by early pioneers
- Focused on **stability and simplicity**

---

## Layer 2 – Merchant and Service Integrations

- Standardized interfaces for **merchants and service providers**
- Payment request formatting and processing
- Integration guidelines for **small and medium businesses**
- Allows merchants to receive payments without complex infrastructure

---

## Layer 3 – Multi-Chain Support

- Expands Fylox beyond Pi Network
- Enables interoperability with other blockchain networks
- Prepares for **resiliency in case of network-specific issues**
- Ensures the protocol is **future-proof**

---

## Layer 4 – AI-driven Governance and Liquidity

- Uses **AI models** to coordinate transaction routing and liquidity management
- Facilitates interactions with larger platforms paying in fiat or other digital assets
- Converts revenues into Pi to distribute to users and merchants
- Supports **real-world utility of Pi** across multiple use cases

---

## Layer 5 – Global Ecosystem and Applications

- Fully integrated Fylox ecosystem
- Supports **peer-to-peer, merchant, digital services, and cross-community transactions**
- Provides standards for third-party developers and platforms
- Enables broader adoption and real-world utility

---

## Key Points

- The **MVP focuses on Layer 1** only
- Layers 2–5 are part of the **strategic roadmap**
- This layered structure ensures **scalability, interoperability, and real-world applicability**
- Provides a **clear vision for developers, merchants, and users**

---

## Disclaimer

Fylox Protocol is currently **experimental**.  
The full 5-layer architecture is **planned for future development**, and not all layers are implemented yet.
